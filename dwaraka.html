Dwaraka:

Dwarkadhish temple, also known as the Jagat Mandir and occasionally spelled Dwarakadheesh, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Krishna, who is worshiped here by the name Dwarkadhish, or 'King of Dwarka'. The temple is located at Dwarka city of Gujarat, India, which is one of the destinations of Char Dham, a Hindu pilgrimage circuit. The main shrine of the five-storied building, supported by 72 pillars, is known as Jagat Mandir or Nija Mandir.As per Hindu legend, Dwarka was built on a piece of land by Krishna that was reclaimed from the sea.

The town of Dwarka in Gujarat has a history that dates back centuries, and mentioned in the Mahabharata epic as the Dwaraka Kingdom. Situated on the banks of river Gomti, the town is described in legend as the capital of Krishna.

Devotional Importance:

Since this site is associated with the ancient city of Dvārakā and the Vedic era Krishna of Mahabharata, it is an important place of pilgrimage for Hindus. It is one of 3 main pilgrimage sites related to "Krishna" circuit, namely 48 kos parikrama of Kurukshetra in Haryana state, Braj Parikarma in Mathura of Uttar Pradesh state and Dwarka Parikrama (Dwarkadish Yatra) at Dwarkadhish Temple in Gujarat state.

According to a legend, Meera Bai, the famed Rajput princess who was also a poetess-saint and a staunch devotee of Krishna, merged with the deity at this temple. It is one of the Sapta Puri, the seven holy cities of India.

Suitable time to visit:

Most of the time it has a pleasant climate. One can also visit Dwarka in summers and do some sightseeing but the heat can tire out the travellers. The winter period is suitable for touring the temples as it is cool at that time.

Location :

Dwarka situated in the west is in the state of Gujarat, India.

How to reach?

Porbandar (95 km) and Jamnagar (145 km) are the closest airports to Dwarka.It takes approximately one hour to reach Jamnagar from Mumbai and two hours from Delhi.

Nearest railway is Dwarka railway station around 3 kilometers from temple.
